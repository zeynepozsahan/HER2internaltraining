Breast HER2 IHC Scoring

Read this before you score — no prior pathology experience required.

Self-study ~15 min read 25-case quiz

🧬 What is HER2?

HER2 (Human Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor 2) is a protein found on the surface of cells that promotes cell growth. In some breast cancers, the HER2 gene is amplified, causing the cell to overproduce the HER2 protein — making the tumor more aggressive.

HER2 status is one of the most important biomarkers in breast oncology because it directly determines treatment eligibility. Drugs like trastuzumab (Herceptin) and trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd / Enhertu) target HER2-expressing tumors. Getting the score right means more patients receive the right treatment.

HER2 is measured using Immunohistochemistry (IHC) — a staining technique that makes HER2 protein visible under a microscope as brown membrane staining on tumor cells.

🔬 HER2 IHC Scoring: The Four Scores

Pathologists assign one of four IHC scores based on the intensity and completeness of membrane staining in invasive tumor cells.

0 (null)
HER2 Null

No staining, or faint/barely perceptible incomplete membrane staining in ≤10% of tumor cells.

0+ (ultralow)
HER2 Ultralow

Faint/barely perceptible incomplete membrane staining in >10% of tumor cells. A new, clinically important category.

1+
HER2 Low

Faint/barely perceptible complete membrane staining in >10% of tumor cells.

2+
HER2 Low or Positive

Weak to moderate complete membrane staining in >10% of cells. Equivocal — requires ISH testing to confirm gene amplification.

3+
HER2 Positive

Strong, complete membrane staining in >10% of tumor cells.

💡 Key distinction: Complete vs. incomplete staining

Complete staining means the entire cell membrane is outlined (like a ring). Incomplete means only part of the membrane stains. This distinction separates 0 from 1+ and is one of the hardest calls in HER2 scoring — and where AI assistance is most valuable.

📊 HER2 Classifications

IHC scores map to clinical classifications that guide treatment decisions:

IHC Score Classification ISH Required?
0 (no membrane staining) HER2 Null No
0 (with membrane staining >10%) HER2 Ultralow No
1+ HER2 Low No
2+ / ISH negative HER2 Low Yes — to confirm
2+ / ISH positive HER2 Positive Yes — confirms
3+ HER2 Positive No

🧪 What is ISH?

ISH (In Situ Hybridization) — often called FISH or CISH — is a molecular test that directly measures HER2 gene copy number. It is used as a reflex test when IHC gives an equivocal 2+ result.

  • ISH positive: HER2 gene is amplified → HER2 Positive
  • ISH negative: HER2 gene is not amplified → HER2 Low (if IHC was 2+)

Some cases will ask you to determine ISH status in addition to the IHC score. You'll see both the staining image and AI overlays to help. ISH questions will only appear for 2+ cases.

✅ Practical Scoring Tips

  • 🔍
    Focus on invasive tumor cells only. Do not score ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS) or stroma.
  • 🟤
    Brown = HER2 staining. The darker and more complete the brown ring around the cell membrane, the higher the score. Blue = nuclei.
  • 📐
    Complete vs. incomplete. Ask: is the membrane fully outlined (like a closed ring) or only partially? Complete + strong = 3+. Complete + weak = 1+. Incomplete + faint = 0+.
  • 📏
    Use the "10% rule". The score applies if >10% of invasive tumor cells show that pattern. A single strongly staining cell doesn't make it a 3+.
  • 🤖
    Use the AI overlay — but question it.
  • 🖼️
    Patches ≠ diagnosis — context is everything. This is especially important for anyone involved in training algorithms. Algorithm training can make use of patches to inform decisions but in real pathology a full slide context is always needed to make an informed diagnostic decision for each patient. That decision can directly change the course of treatment — whether someone is HER2 null, ultralow, low, or 3+ determines what therapy they receive. Use the whole-slide context when to understand what you're looking at: what is invasive tumor vs. normal tissue, what is in situ tumor (DCIS), and how to distinguish HER2 null from ultralow — one of the hardest and most consequential calls in HER2 scoring.

⚠️ Important Context

The goal of this training is not to turn you into a pathologist — it's to give you enough fluency to understand how an IHC image is scored, how a diagnosis is made, and what our AI is doing for pathologists who are scoring these images or learning to score. You should also come away able to recognise common failure modes of the AI — be aware that even our AI can make mistakes.
